The Great Rift Valley

Known as the "Cradle of Mankind," the Afar region in Ethiopia is the world's most significant site for studying human evolution.

Dinknesh: "You are Marvelous"

Discovered in 1974, Lucy (Australopithecus afarensis) lived 3.2 million years ago and proved that our ancestors walked upright.

Ardi: 4.4 Million Years Old

Ardipithecus ramidus (Ardi) pushed back the human timeline even further, offering a glimpse into life before Lucy.

Ancient Technology

Ethiopia is home to the world's oldest stone tools (2.6 million years old), marking the dawn of human innovation and culture.
