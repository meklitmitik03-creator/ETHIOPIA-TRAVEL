Enkutatash (New Year)

Celebrated on September 11th, marking the end of the rainy season with yellow daisies (Adey Abeba).

Timket (Epiphany)

The most important Orthodox festival, commemorating the baptism of Jesus in the Jordan River.

Genna (Ledet)

Ethiopian Christmas celebrated on January 7th with traditional games like Yegenna Chewata.

Fasika (Tensae)

Ethiopian Easter, following a 55-day fast. A time of massive family feasts and Doro Wat.

Siklet (Good Friday)

A day of deep prayer and fasting, commemorating the crucifixion of Jesus.

Eid al-Fitr

Celebrating the end of Ramadan with prayers, charity, and communal feasts across the nation.

Eid al-Adha (Arefa)

The Feast of Sacrifice, honoring the willingness of Ibrahim to obey Allah.

Meskel

The Finding of the True Cross, famous for the giant Demera bonfire and yellow flowers.
