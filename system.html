The Calendar

The Orthodox system follows the Ge'ez calendar, consisting of 13 months—12 months of 30 days and a 13th month (Pagume) of 5 or 6 days. We are currently 7 to 8 years behind the Gregorian calendar.

Sacred Ge'ez

Ge'ez is the liturgical language of the Tewahedo Church. As one of the world's oldest Semitic languages, it serves as the foundation for the Qene (poetry) and Deggua (hymns) that define Orthodox worship.

The Great Fasts

The Orthodox system mandates nearly 250 fasting days a year. During these times, no animal products (meat, dairy, eggs) are consumed, leading to Ethiopia's world-famous vegan culinary heritage.

Zema & Chant

Invented by Saint Yared in the 6th century, the musical notation system (Zema) consists of three modes: Ge’ez, Ezel, and Araray, representing the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

The Holy Tabot

Central to every church is the Tabot, a replica of the Tablets of Law. It is the presence of the Tabot that consecrates a building as a church, linking Ethiopia to the ancient Solomonic tradition.
