Lalibela

Carved from solid volcanic tuff in the 12th century, these eleven monolithic churches represent a pinnacle of human engineering. Legend tells that while men worked by day, angels continued the construction by night.

Debre Damo Gedam

Perched atop a sheer-sided cliff (amba), this 6th-century monastery is accessible only by climbing a 15-meter leather rope. It remains one of the oldest standing Christian structures in the world, preserving ancient manuscripts and Aksumite architecture.

Bet Giyorgis

The most famous of the rock-hewn churches, the Church of St. George is carved in the shape of a perfect Greek cross. It stands 12 meters deep within its pit, connected to the other churches by a labyrinth of dark tunnels.

Lake Tana

Lake Tana hides over 30 island monasteries, such as Ura Kidane Mehret. These secluded sanctuaries served as hiding places for the Ark of the Covenant and royal treasures during times of conflict, housing centuries of vibrant ecclesiastical art.

Aksum Tsion

The Church of Our Lady Mary of Zion is the most sacred site in Ethiopia. According to Tewahedo tradition, the Chapel of the Tablet within the compound houses the original Ark of the Covenant, brought from Jerusalem by Menelik I.
